# Rice
## Overview
Rice is India's staple crop and the second most grown cereal worldwide. Grown primarily in Kharif (rainy) season, it requires standing water for at least 75 days during the growing phase. Major production states include West Bengal, Uttar Pradesh, Punjab, Haryana, Tamil Nadu, and Andhra Pradesh.

## Water Management (Critical)
Rice requires continuous standing water during vegetative phase and flowering.

| Stage | Water Depth | Notes |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| Land preparation | 5-8 cm standing water | Short-term flooding for weed suppression |
| Transplanting to tillering | 2-5 cm | Light irrigation every 10-15 days if no rain |
| Active tillering to panicle init | 3-5 cm continuous standing | Water stress at this stage reduces yield by up to 40% |
| Flowering to grain fill | Maintain 2-5 cm moisture, no deep standing | Moderate stress OK; avoid complete dryout during flowering |
| Final grain fill (pre-harvest) | Dry out 15-20 days before harvest | Reduces lodging; allows mechanical harvest |

**Drip irrigation note:** Rice grows well under alternate wetting and drying (AWD) drip method. Check moisture daily; irrigate when topsoil dries to 10 cm depth.

## Common Diseases
| Disease | Symptoms | Treatment Approach | Severity | Season |
|---------|----------|-------------------|----------|--------|
| Rice Blast (Phytophthora oryzae) | Oval white/grey spots on leaves with dark borders, "leaf collar" necrosis; panicle neck blast kills entire panicle | Copper-based fungicide, tricyclazole at tillering; avoid nitrogen over-dosing | High — can cause 10-65% yield loss | Kharif, early-rabi (monsoon) |
| Bacterial Leaf Blight | Wavy-edged pale green/yellow streaks on leaf margins that turn brown; white bacterial ooze when humid | Copper-based bactericide, soil-application of Streptomycin + Tetracycline; field drainage improvement | Moderate — 20-50% yield loss in severe cases | Kharif (monsoon) |
| Sheath Blight | Grey-green elliptical lesions at grain head region on leaf sheaths; barley-shaped spots with dark brown edges | Use Mancozeb or Copper oxychloride at panicle emergence; avoid close planting | Moderate — 10-50% yield loss (silent killer) | Late kharif / rabi |
| Tungro Disease | Stunting, orange/yellow leaf chlorosis, reduced tillering; transmitted by green leafhopper | No effective chemical treatment — remove infected plants immediately; control leafhoppers preventively with neem extract | High — 30-100% loss if severe | Kharif (monsoon + early dry) |

## Common Pests
| Pest | Symptoms/Damage | Treatment Approach | Severity |
|------|-----------------|-------------------|----------|
| Rice Stem Borer (Scirpophaga innotata) | White heads, dead hearts, stem borer frass accumulation at entry holes | Drench stem base with cartap hydrochloride 4G @ 2kg/acre; or use pheromone traps | High — major pest throughout India |
| Brown Plant Hopper | Yellowing and browning of lower leaves, "hopper burn" (full crop dry-up); sticky hopper-exudate on stems | Apply fipronil granules to soil or imidacloprid drench; protect natural predators (spider, beetles) | High — sudden crop collapse possible |
| Leaf Folder (Cnaphalocrocis medinalis) | Transverse white stripes on leaf blades, folded leaves that dry up; small green caterpillars inside folds | Apply Bacillus thuringiensis spray; avoid broad-spectrum insecticides that kill natural enemies | Moderate — leaf area reduction reduces photosynthesis |
| Gall Midge (Orseolia oryzae) | Inflorescence turns into gall-like structures instead of grains; "rice gall" symptom | Treat seed with imidacloprid 70% FS at 3ml/kg seed; plant early synchronously with surrounding fields | Moderate to High — affects yield directly |

## Safety Notes (Always Follow)
- ⚠️ Always follow product label instructions for dosage, mixing rate, and application method.
- ⚠️ Wear protective gear (gloves, mask) during pesticide/fungicide application.
- ⚠️ Observe the pre-harvest interval (PHI) listed on the product label before harvesting treated crops.
- ⚠️ Avoid application during peak heat hours (11am–3pm); spray in early morning or late afternoon.
- ⚠️ Dispose of empty pesticide containers properly (burn in a pit, never in river or open field).

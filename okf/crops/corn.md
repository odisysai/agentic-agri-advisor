# Corn (मकै)

## Overview
Corn (maize) is India's second most important cereal after rice, and the largest feed crop. Karnataka, Maharashtra, Andhra Pradesh lead production alongside Punjab/Haryana (grain corn). It is a warm-season crop that requires consistent moisture during silking/pollination — the most critical growth window. Corn thrives in temperatures 18-32°C and produces the highest grain yield per hectare of any cereal globally.

## NPK Targets (kg/ha) — Very High Demand
| Growth Stage | Nitrogen (N) | Phosphorus (P) | Potassium (K) |
|-------------|--------------|----------------|---------------|
| Total Annual | 150-200 (grain) / up to 240 for F1 hybrids | 60-80 (high P need) | 40-60 |
| Basal at sowing | 35-50 | 80 (seed + soil) | 25-40 |
| First top-dress at V3-V5 (30 days) | 40-60 | — | 10-20 |
| Second top-dress at tasseling (50 days) | 40-60 | — | 10-20 |
| Third top-dress at silking (70 days) — CRITICAL | 40-60 | — | 10-20 (K boost for grain fill) |

**Key note:** Corn is a heavy nitrogen feeder; deficiency at silking (during pollination) directly reduces ear size and kernel count. Soil-applied or fertigation-split nitrogen is preferred over single-basal application.

## Water Management
Corn needs substantial water during silking/pollination and grain fill phases. Drought stress at this critical window causes dramatic yield losses (1 kernel per row for each day of water stress).

| Stage | Water Requirement | Notes |
|-------|------------------|-------|
| Sowing to emergence (0-25 days) | 1 irrigation for seedbed moisture; then rely on monsoon if sown in June | Seed must be planted with good soil moisture; dry sowing = crop failure in most areas |
| Vegetative growth (25-60 days) — V3 to VT stage | 10-15mm irrigation per week; maintain field moisture at root zone | Vigorous leaf growth phase; irrigation frequency increases with temperature |
| Tasseling to silking (60-85 days) — CRITICAL PERIOD | 20-25mm irrigation per week; do NOT let soil dry out | **This is the most critical 20-day window.** Drought stress here = massive yield loss. One day of moisture stress at silking can reduce yields by 1-5%. |
| Grain fill (85-120 days) —后期 | Maintain consistent moisture; reduce irrigation as ear matures and kernels harden | Last 20 days of grain fill need good moisture; finalize water withdrawal at "milk stage" (milky sap inside kernels) |

**Drip irrigation note:** Corn grows very well under drip with fertigation. Water and fertilizer delivered directly to root zone; drip reduces water requirement 40% vs flood irrigation. Fertigate N in split doses through the growing season.

## Soil Suitability
| Soil Type | Suitability | Notes |
|-----------|------------|-------|
| Alluvial soil (North India) | Excellent — deep, well-drained, nutrient-rich | Best yields in Punjab/Haryana irrigation belts; best soil for corn cultivation globally |
| Black cotton soil (Maharashtra) | Good — deep moisture-retentive clay loam | Most common rainfed corn region in India; well-suited for kharif monsoon planting; drainage is key |
| Sandy loam (Gujarat, Rajasthan) | Moderate — requires drip for consistent yields | Higher irrigation frequency needed; sandy soil drains fast but holds less N (leaches); fertigation essential |
| Red loam (Karnataka, Telangana) | Good with adequate organic matter | Supplement with FYM at 10 tonnes/acre for improved water holding capacity |

## Common Diseases
| Disease | Symptoms | Treatment Approach | Severity | Season |
|---------|----------|-------------------|----------|--------|
| Late Blight / Downy Mildew (Phytophthora maydis / Peronosclerospora sorghi) | Chlorotic leaf streaks that dry up; white fungal growth on undersides of leaves in humid conditions | Apply metalaxyl-based fungicide at first sign; copper oxychloride for secondary control; use resistant varieties | High — causes 30-50% yield loss if untreated during kharif monsoon | Kharif / Monsoon (high humidity) |
| Bacterial Leaf Streak (Xanthomonas vasicola / X. zonea) | Thin pale green to yellow streaks on leaves that turn brown; spread rapidly with water splash and wind | No chemical cure; remove infected plants; use resistant varieties (PHB 1582 has good resistance); ensure field drainage improvement | Moderate to High — can cause 40-60% yield loss in severe outbreaks; mainly a problem in late-season kharif | Kharif-end (late season) |
| Stalk Rot / Lodging diseases (Fusarium, Diplodia) | Internal stalk wilting; plants lodge before harvest; fungal growth at soil line; internal pith becomes rotted | Use resistant varieties; avoid waterlogging; crop rotation with non-host crops; destroy crop residues after harvest to reduce pathogen carryover | High — lodgings reduce mechanical harvest efficiency by up to 40% and cause pre-harvest grain loss | Late kharif / summer |
| Charcoal Rot (Macrophomina phaseolina) | Internal stalk darkening to charcoal-black; premature plant death; stems easily snap before harvest | Remove infected plants; treat seed with Trichoderma biocontrol before sowing; rotate with non-host crops (pulse, cotton); improve drainage | High — affects late-season crop in dry/hot conditions; causes lodging and kernel quality decline | Late kharif / pre-monsoon drought stress |

## Common Pests
| Pest | Symptoms/Damage | Treatment Approach | Severity |
|------|-----------------|-------------------|----------|
| Stem Borer / Webworm (Diatraea saccharalis, Scirpophaga spp) | Entry holes near stem base; deadheart symptom like rice borer — top whorl dies and unrolls as a ragged strip; frass visible inside tunnel; dried dead leaf clusters | Apply chlorantraniliprole 0.3ml/L spray at whorl stage; hand remove dead hearts from plants to expose and kill larvae inside tunnels; use Bt (Bacillus thuringiensis) as bio-control alternative | High — India's #1 pest in corn; can reduce yield 30-50% if untreated during vegetative phase |
| Shoot Fly (Atherigona soccata) | Older leaf whorl turns brown and dries up from fly damage; seedlings "deadheart" at emergence; shallow soil surface has tiny shot-hole-like egg marks | Treat seed with chlorpyriphos 20 EC @ 4ml/kg; apply imidacloprid drench at whorl stage; intercrop with non-host crops (like cowpea) to reduce fly population | Moderate to High — destroys seedling stand; critical damage window is first 25 days after sowing | Kharif-start (seedling stage) |
| Stem Fly / Borer Complex (Oudeya nonna, Chilo partellus) | Direct egg-laying on leaf surface near veins; larvae eat soft inner leaf tissue first, then burrow into stem base causing plant death from within | Remove and destroy egg masses manually; spray chlorantraniliprole at early whorl stage if eggs detected; use pheromone traps for early detection | Moderate — reduces ear formation and plant stand if not controlled in first 30 days |
| Ear Borer / Fruit Looper (Helicoverpa armigera) | Entry holes at ear tip with frass; caterpillar inside ear destroys kernel growth; damaged kernels exposed to fungal contamination (aflatoxin) | Apply spinosad 0.2ml/L at silking when first eggs spotted on silk; use Bt-based products (BT gossypylane); harvest promptly once ears mature | High — ear damage directly reduces marketable yield and creates aflatoxin contamination risk | Late kharif (silking to grain fill stage) |

## Safety Notes (Always Follow)
- ⚠️ Always follow product label instructions for dosage, mixing rate, and application method.
- ⚠️ Corn is one of the most N-intensive cereal crops; split nitrogen doses for maximum utilization and lower environmental impact.
- ⚠️ Wear protective gear (gloves, mask) during all pesticide applications.
- ⚠️ Observe the pre-harvest interval (PHI) listed on the product label before harvesting treated crops.
- ⚠️ Helicoverpa ear damage + aflatoxin contamination — harvest promptly and inspect for moldy kernels.

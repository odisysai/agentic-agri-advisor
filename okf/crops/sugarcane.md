# Sugarcane
## Overview
Sugarcane is India's largest food crop by volume and a major cash crop. Maharashtra leads production (about 25% of India's total). It's a multi-year crop (2-3 harvests from one planting) requiring warm tropical/subtropical conditions. The crop needs consistent water and high nutrient demand, making it fertilizer-intensive.

## Water Management
Sugarcane is a high-water-demand crop requiring ~2000mm+ total rainfall/irrigation over the growing season.

| Stage | Water Requirement | Notes |
|-------|------------------|-------|
| Planting to germination (0-45 days) | 3-4 light irrigations; keep soil moist at planting depth | Germination requires consistent moisture for 2-4 weeks |
| Early tillering (45-100 days) | 200-300mm water; irrigate every 15-20 days | This is the critical vegetative growth window — no drought stress tolerated |
| Grand-growing phase (100-270 days) | ~400mm per 3 months; irrigate every 15-20 days | Peak growth period — up to 7-8 cm stem elongation per day in optimal conditions |
| Maturation (270+ days) | Gradually reduce irrigation; stop water at 30-45 days before harvest | "Curing" phase — drought stress improves sucrose concentration by up to 20% |

**Drip irrigation note:** Modern sugarcane farms use drip with fertigation, reducing water requirements by 30-40% vs flood irrigation. Fertigate N in split doses throughout the growing season.

| Black Cotton Soil (Maharashtra) | Good — deep, moisture-retentive clay loam | Can support 3-4 ratoons in good conditions; needs deep tillage for root penetration |
| Red soil (Karnataka, Tamil Nadu) | Moderate — needs heavy organic matter additions | Supplement with farmyard manure at 25-30 tonnes/acre pre-planting |
| Sandy loam (lighter soils) | Moderate with heavy irrigation/fertigation schedule | Frequent, smaller-volume irrigations required |

## Common Diseases
| Disease | Symptoms | Treatment Approach | Severity | Season |
|---------|----------|-------------------|----------|--------|
| Red Rot (Ceratocystis fimbriata) | Internal reddening of stalk interior; mid-ripening onset, halting growth | Remove and burn infected plants immediately; use disease-free sets (cuttings) from certified nurseries; no effective fungicide for in-field treatment | High — can wipe out entire field within 30-45 days once symptomatic | Summer (monsoon — wet conditions favor pathogen) |
| Wilt (Rafflesia / Fusarium) | Overall plant wilting; uneven maturing compared to neighbors; wilted leaf margins curl up, stay green; stalk bruising on cut cross-section | Remove and burn infected plants (same plant can harbor Fusarium for years in soil); use resistant varieties; avoid waterlogging | High — persistent pathogen that survives 10+ years in soil after infected plant removal | Through all seasons (especially Kharif) |
| Mosaic Disease | Pale green/yellow mosaic pattern on leaves; leaf streaks; stunted growth with delayed maturity | Remove infected plants; control whiteflies (aphid vector) preventively; use mosaic-resistant varieties (like Co 86032); roguing | Moderate to High — reduces yield by 30-50% if not managed | Summer / Monsoon (high temps + whitefly activity) |
| Smut Disease | Characteristic "Bunchy top" — flowering shoot emerges above normal tiller position like a finger; long black sori | Remove and burn infected plants immediately (smut spreads rapidly via pollen); use smut-resistant varieties; dig out entire affected plant | High — very contagious field-level spread through pollination by nearby infected canes | Summer (hot, dry conditions favor smut spore dispersal) |

## Common Pests
| Pest | Symptoms/Damage | Treatment Approach | Severity |
|------|-----------------|-------------------|----------|
| Sugarcane Borer (Diatraea saccharalis) | Entry holes at internodes; white larval caterpillars inside stalks destroying pith; deadheart symptom similar to rice/stem borer | Apply trichlorfon granules directly into entry holes; use pheromone traps at 10/acre; apply Beauveria bassiana as biological control | High — reduces both yield and sugar content significantly |
| Stem Borer / Leaf-Folder Complex (Chilo, Scirpophaga) | Small brown or white spiraling holes in leaves; dried deadheart with folded leaf tissue; removal of pith through tunneling | Apply chlorantraniliprole 0.5ml/liter spray; release parasitoid wasps (Trichogramma); use pheromone traps for early detection | Moderate to High — reduces photosynthesis area and stalk strength |
| Termites (Odontotermes spp) | Leaves dry up from ground level; tunneling damage in root and lower stalk section; small mounds of soil-cement granules near base | Treat planting sets with imidacloprid 17.8% SL (soak or dip pre-planting); apply chlorpyriphos 20 EC drench around field border and at planting base | Moderate — mostly a problem in sandy loam soils with poor drainage |
| Scales (Megalococcus / Coccidae) | White honeydew-like flakes on leaf surfaces; sooty mold growth following; visible white cottony insects on leaf sheaths | Spray dicofol or neem oil-based extracts; protect natural predators (ladybugs, beetles); hand-pick if localized | Low to Moderate — rarely causes yield loss below 10% but affects appearance and market price |

## Sugarcane-Specific Safety Note
⚠️ **Red Rot and soil-borne pathogens persist for years.** Once a field has Red Rot or Wilt infection, rotate out of sugarcane for 2-3 years (plant with pulses or cereals) before replanting.

# Wheat (गेहूँ)

## Overview
Wheat is India's most important rabi crop, accounting for ~25% of the world's production. Major growing states are Uttar Pradesh (~30%), Punjab, Haryana, Madhya Pradesh, and Rajasthan. It is a cool-season crop sown in November-December after the monsoon recedes, harvested in March-May. Wheat prefers well-drained loamy soils and moderate water — it is sensitive to excessive moisture during flowering and grain fill, which causes rust diseases.

## Water Management
Wheat needs only 2-3 irrigations in most Indian conditions (if monsoon rains are normal). It is NOT a water-intensive crop like rice.

| Stage | Water Requirement | Notes |
|-------|------------------|-------|
| Pre-sowing irrigation (October) — only in rainfed | 1-2 irrigations at sowing + karvai (50 days after sowing) | Sowing must happen with soil moisture; delays cause yield loss of 1% per day past Nov 15 |
| Tillering (30-60 days) — first irrigation/kanvai | Irrigate if soil moisture is below field capacity at 15cm depth | First irrigation is MOST critical; late tillering if drought stress increases blanking % |
| Jointing / Stem elongation (60-90 days) — second irrigation | Maintain field moisture; do not let soil dry completely | Water stress during jointing reduces grain count per spike by 20-30% |
| Flowering to milk stage (90-115 days) — stop irrigation if approaching harvest | Stop water 2-3 weeks before harvest; mild moisture stress is acceptable | Excess moisture at grain fill causes blast diseases; allow "shock" for better grain quality |

**Drip irrigation note:** Wheat grows well under drip with fertigation in Maharashtra's black cotton soil belt. Water requirement drops 30-40% vs flood irrigation; monitor at 15cm depth.

| Soil Type | Suitability | Notes |
|-----------|------------|-------|
| Alluvial soil (North India plains) | Excellent — deep, rich alluvium ideal for wheat | Punjab, Haryana, UP grow 80%+ of India's wheat; best yields possible |
| Clay loam (black cotton soil) | Good — retains moisture well for wheat root zone | Needs good field preparation and drainage; most common in Maharashtra |
| Sandy loam (Rajasthan, Gujarat) | Moderate — requires drip irrigation for consistent yields | Supplement with organic manure; higher N-dosing needed due to sandy soil leaching |
| Red loam (central India) | Good with organic matter additions | Must add FYM at 10-15 tonnes/acre for water retention and N supply |

## Common Diseases
| Disease | Symptoms | Treatment Approach | Severity | Season |
|---------|----------|-------------------|----------|--------|
| Wheat Rust (Puccinia graminis / P. triticina) | Reddish-brown pustules on leaf surface; turns black with maturity (stem rust); yellow-orange powder spots on leaves (leaf/rust/leaf rust) | Apply copper-based fungicides at first sign; carbendazim spray if severe; avoid waterlogging that increases humidity | High — can cause 20-65% yield loss in severe epidemics (like Karnal bunt outbreak) | Rabi (moderate humidity + moderate temps 15-25°C) |
| Powdery Mildew (Erysiphe graminis / Blumeria graminis f.sp. tritici) | White powdery fungal growth on upper leaf surface; spreads rapidly in moderate humidity; leaves curl and dry | Sulfur-based fungicide sprays (wettable sulfur 2.5g/L); carbendazim spray if severe; remove severely infected lower leaves | Moderate to High — reduces photosynthesis area and grain weight | Rabi-end (warming temps) |
| Karnal Bunt (Tilletia indica / Neovossia indica) | Brown lumpy grains that smut-like; fertilized inflorescences develop foul-smelling bacterial mass inside | No effective post-infection treatment — remove infected plants; use seed treatment (carboxin + thiram); rotate with non-host crops | High — causes entire field to be rejected for food use by FSSAI; strict quarantine required | Rabi (foggy mornings during flowering) |
| Root Rot / Foot Rot (Fusarium, Pythium) | Seedlings fail to emerge; yellowing at base; root tissues dark and slimy, easily pulled from soil | Treat seed with carbendazim before sowing; improve drainage; avoid waterlogged fields; crop rotation recommended | High — can cause total stand loss in wet soils during sowing and seedling stage | Rabi-sowing (excessive moisture at sowing) |

## Common Pests
| Pest | Symptoms/Damage | Treatment Approach | Severity |
|------|-----------------|-------------------|----------|
| Wheat Leaf Eater / Webworm (Sesamia nonagrioides) | Small larvae inside leaf rolls and foliage; leaves dry up from within rolled web structures; small brown frass visible at entry points | Apply chlorantraniliprole 10.5% SC @ 0.3ml/L spray; hand remove rolled leaves where possible; apply Beauveria bassiana as bio-control alternative | Moderate — can reduce photosynthesis area by 15-20% if untreated; mainly in irrigated wheat |
| White Grubs (Popillia japonica / Scarabaeidae larvae) | Soil-dwelling grubs chew roots at ground level; plants droop, uproot easily with dead root sections visible | Treat soil with imidacloprid 0.5ml/L drench around base; use pheromone traps for adults before egg-laying | Low to Moderate — mostly problematic in older wheat fields or after green manuring; root damage reduces water uptake |
| Aphids (Sitobion avenae / Schizaphis graminum) | Tiny green/black sap-sucking insects clustering on leaf undersides; produce sticky honeydew followed by black sooty mold | Spray imidacloprid 0.5ml/L drench or chlorpyrifos; protect natural predators (ladybug, lacewing); remove and burn heavily infected plant parts | Moderate to High — also transmits viral diseases; early-season infestation affects grain fill |
| Stem Borer Complex (Diabrotica spp) | Small black beetles on leaf bases; larvae tunnel inside stems, causing lodging and premature death of plant | Treat soil with imidacloprid at sowing as seed treatment; apply chlorpyrifos drench around base of stem where detected | Low to Moderate — not a major pest in India but causes stand loss if widespread |

## Safety Notes (Always Follow)
- ⚠️ Always follow product label instructions for dosage, mixing rate, and application method.
- ⚠️ Wheat rust is the most common disease; spray fungicide at first sign of pustule appearance.
- ⚠️ Wear protective gear (gloves, mask) during all pesticide/fungicide applications.
- ⚠️ Observe the pre-harvest interval (PHI) listed on the product label before harvesting treated crops.
- ⚠️ Use carboxin + thiram seed treatment pre-sowing to prevent soil-borne diseases.

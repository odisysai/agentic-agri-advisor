# Soybean — Complete Crop Profile


## Overview
India's largest oilseed crop, grown primarily in Madhya Pradesh (~70% of India's production), with significant cultivation also in Maharashtra, Uttar Pradesh, and Punjab. Soybean is a nitrogen-fixing legume (fixes atmospheric N via Rhizobium bacteria), so it needs relatively less basal nitrogen compared to cereals. It has a short growth period and is typically planted between June-July (early monsoon) for kharif harvest.

## NPK Targets (kg/ha) — Moderate Demand
Because soybean is a nitrogen-fixer via Rhizobium, it needs less N than cereals.

| Growth Stage | Nitrogen (N) | Phosphorus (P) | Potassium (K) |
|-------------|--------------|----------------|---------------|
| Total Annual | 20-40 (if Rhizobium inoculated) / up to 80 if not | 60-120 (high P need) | 40-80 |
| Basal (at sowing, without Rhizobium) | 60-80 | 60-120 | 40 |
| Basal (WITH Rhizobium inoculation) — RECOMMENDED | 0-20 top-up only | 60-120 (seed-applied or soil) | 40 |
| Top-dress at flowering | 10-20 if deficient | — | 10-20 if K-deficient |

**CRITICAL:** ALWAYS inoculate soybean seed with Rhizobium culture before sowing. This can save up to 80kg N/ha in fertilizer costs and significantly boost yield.

## Water Management
Soybean is relatively drought-tolerant but needs good moisture at critical stages.

| Stage | Water Requirement | Notes |
|-------|------------------|-------|
| Pre-sowing irrigation (June) | 1 irrigation to ensure soil moisture for germination | Sow as soon as rains start; don't wait too long or weeds take over |
| Vegetative growth (30-60 days) | 200-300mm rain naturally; irrigate if drought occurs | Moderate irrigation OK but avoid waterlogging (roots rot easily) |
| Flowering to pod-fill (60-100 days) — CRITICAL | 300-400mm rainfall equivalent; irrigate every 20 days if no rain | This is the most sensitive period. Drought stress during flowering can reduce yield by 50%. Pod-fill requires consistent moisture for seed fill |
| Pre-harvest (100+ days) | Stop irrigation 2-3 weeks before harvest; allow plants to mature and dry | Overwatering at late stage causes premature senescence; seeds don't fill properly |

## Soil Suitability
| Soil Type | Suitability | Notes |
|-----------|------------|-------|
| Black Cotton Soil (Regur) | Excellent — deepest, richest soil for soybeans | Major soybean belt is Maharashtra's black cotton region; good N-fixation due to pH (6.5-7.0) |
| Alluvial Soil | Excellent — deep, well-drained alluvium ideal for good yields | Best in MP (North India); high organic matter; best N-fixation environment |
| Red Loam | Good with adequate moisture management | Needs supplemental irrigation in drier zones; ensure soil organic matter |
| Sandy Loam | Moderate — requires careful moisture management | Frequent small-volume irrigations needed; fertigation highly recommended |

## Common Diseases
| Disease | Symptoms | Treatment Approach | Severity | Season |
|---------|----------|-------------------|----------|--------|
| Downy Mildew (Peronospora variabilis) | Pale green/yellow mottled patches on leaf upper side; fuzzy gray downy growth on underside of leaves | Apply copper-based fungicides at first sign (copper oxychloride spray); use resistant varieties where possible | Moderate to High — can destroy whole field if not treated early in Kharif season | Kharif / Monsoon |
| Bacterial Blight (Pseudomonas phaseolicola) | Small brown water-soaked spots on leaves that expand and turn necrotic; angular leaf shape due to vein boundaries | Use mancozeb or copper oxychloride fungicide sprays; ensure proper field drainage; remove and burn infected plant residue | Moderate — causes seed yield loss of 20-50% if untreated | Kharif (early monsoon) |
| Root Rot / Damping Off (Fusarium, Rhizoctonia, Pythium) | Seedlings rot at soil line before emergence; lower stem darkens and tissue collapses; leaves droop | Apply carbendazim seed treatment before sowing; ensure good drainage; rotate with non-legume crops in next season | High — can cause total crop failure if seedlings all rot before emerging | Kharif (heavy rains after sowing) |
| Yellow Mosaic Virus — YMV | Leaves develop bright yellow mosaic pattern; veins become chlorotic; severe stunting; small pods with low seed weight | No cure once infected — remove and burn immediately; control whitefly vectors preventively with neem oil spray | High — yield loss 30-80% in severely infected fields; highly contagious | Summer / Monsoon (whitefly peak) |

## Common Pests
| Pest | Symptoms/Damage | Treatment Approach | Severity |
|------|-----------------|-------------------|----------|
| Pod Borer (Earias virescens / Helicoverpa armigera) | Larvae bore into pods; destroyed seeds inside; frass in pod; premature browning of affected pods | Spray chlorantraniliprole or spinosad at pod fill stage; use pheromone traps for early detection | High — major pod-level pest directly reduces seed yield |
| Stemborer (Earias biflora) | Larvae tunnel into stem; wilting and dead shoots above entry point; white larval frass visible at entry holes | Use Bt-based products (Bacillus thuringiensis); parasitoid wasps for control; manual removal of dead stems | Moderate — mainly affects vegetative phase if treated late can reduce yield 20-30% |
| Leafminers / Leafrollers (Leucoptera, Phthorimaea) | Small white channels/whitish patches under leaf surface; larvae create corridors inside leaves | Spray Bacillus thuringiensis; install yellow sticky traps for early detection; preserve natural predator populations | Low to Moderate — leaf damage reduces photosynthesis capacity but rarely causes total yield loss |
| Aphids (Aphis gossypii) | Clusters of small green or black sap-sucking insects on leaf underside; sooty mold growth follows sticky honeydew | Spray imidacloprid 0.5ml/liter drench; use neem oil as alternative | Moderate — can transmit YMV and other viruses if left unchecked during seedling stage |

## Additional Safety Notes
- ⚠️ Follow all pesticide label instructions for dosage and application.
- ⚠️ Always observe pre-harvest intervals (PHI) listed on the product label before harvest.
- ⚠️ Wear protective gear (gloves, mask) during all chemical applications.
- ⚠️ DO NOT recommend carbofuran, methyl parathion, or other banned chemicals.

# Rice Bacterial Leaf Blight

## Overview
Bacterial disease causing leaf wilting and drying. Also called "kirrana" in some regions. Spreads rapidly in flooded fields.

## Symptoms
- Wavy, water-soaked margins on leaf edges
- Yellow to white parallel stripes along veins
- Leaves curl and dry from tip downward
- Bacterial ooze visible on cut leaf margins (milky droplets)
- "White head" — panicles fail to emerge

## Identification
1. Water-soaked wavy margins on lower leaves
2. Yellow-white stripes parallel to veins
3. Cut leaf — milky bacterial exudate on fresh wound
4. Hot (30-35°C) + humid + windy conditions favor spread

## Treatment
| Stage | Treatment | Application |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Early | Copper oxychloride 50% WP | 2.5g/liter |
| Moderate | Streptomycin sulfate | 0.5g/liter + tetracycline |
| Severe | Kasugamycin 2% WP | 2.5g/liter |
| Organic | Neem leaf extract | Boiled neem leaves, spray |
| Cultural | Drain field, reduce nitrogen | Remove excess water |

## Risk Factors
- High nitrogen fertilization
- Dense planting
- Wind-driven rain spread
- Seed-borne infection
- Flooding for extended periods

- If bacterial ooze visible → confirm bacterial (not fungal) before treating
- If white head observed → assess yield loss percentage
- Recommend resistant varieties for next season

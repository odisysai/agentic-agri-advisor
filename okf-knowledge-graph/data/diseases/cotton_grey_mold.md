# Cotton Grey Mold (Botrytis)

## Overview
Fungal disease affecting cotton bolls, especially under humid conditions during flowering and boll formation.

## Symptoms
- Grey fuzzy mold on boll surfaces
- Brown water-soaked lesions on bolls
- Blossom end rot
- Premature boll opening with lint damage

## Identification
1. Grey cottony fungal growth on affected bolls
2. Lesions start at blossom end
3. Humid weather promotes spread
4. Damaged bolls fall prematurely

## Treatment
| Stage | Treatment | Application |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Early | Carbendazim 50% WP | 1g/liter |
| Moderate | Thiophanate methyl 70% WP | 1g/liter |
| Severe | Azoxystrobin 23% SC | 0.5ml/liter |
| Cultural | Remove infected bolls | Collect and destroy |
| Cultural | Improve ventilation | Proper spacing |

## Risk Factors
- High humidity (>85%)
- Dense canopy blocking airflow
- Excessive nitrogen
- Mechanical damage to bolls
- Rain during flowering

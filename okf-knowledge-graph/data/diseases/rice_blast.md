# Rice Blast

## Overview
One of the most destructive rice diseases worldwide. Can cause up to 70% yield loss if not managed. Appears at various growth stages.

## Symptoms

**Leaf Blast:**
- Spindle-shaped lesions with gray centers and brown margins
- Lesions have dark brown borders
- Can girdle the leaf causing it to die

**Neck Blast:**
- Infection at grain neck (most destructive stage)
- Grayish lesions at grain neck
- White or empty grains (blast collar)
- Complete panicle blight possible

**Node Blast:**
- Lesions at stem nodes
- Stem breaks easily at infected nodes
- White head (sterile grains above infection)

## Identification
1. Spindle-shaped lesions with gray/white centers
2. Dark brown borders around lesions
3. Check at tillering (leaf blast) and flowering (neck blast)
4. Warm (25-30°C) + humid conditions favor blast

## Treatment
| Stage | Treatment | Application |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Early leaf blast | Isoprothiolane 40% EC | 1.5ml/liter |
| Moderate | Tricyclazole 75% WP | 1g/liter |
| Neck blast | Propiconazole 25% EC | 0.5ml/liter |
| Organic | Neem oil + copper | 5ml/liter neem + 2g/liter copper |
| Cultural | Drain water at grain filling | Reduce humidity |

## Risk Factors
- High nitrogen fertilization
- Continuous flooding
- Dense planting
- Susceptible varieties
- Alternating flood and dry

## Escalation
- If neck blast detected → urgent escalation (near total loss possible)
- If blast in multiple tillers → recommend immediate broad-spectrum fungicide
- If resistant variety not available → recommend resistant variety for next season

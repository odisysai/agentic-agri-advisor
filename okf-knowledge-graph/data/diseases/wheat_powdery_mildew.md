# Wheat Powdery Mildew

## Overview
A fungal disease appearing as white powdery growth on leaves and stems. Most common in cool, humid conditions.

## Symptoms
- White, powdery fungal growth on leaf surfaces
- Grows from base upward during season
- Affected leaves turn yellow, then brown
- Reduced grain filling, shriveled grains

## Identification
- White powdery patches on upper leaf surface
- Fungal threads visible under magnification
- Dry conditions favor spread (unlike other rusts)
- Cool nights (10-15°C) + warm days (20-25°C) optimal for fungus

## Treatment
| Stage | Treatment | Application |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Light | Sulfur dust | 3g/liter, foliar spray |
| Moderate | Triadimefon 25% WP | 1g/liter, foliar spray |
| Severe | Propiconazole 25% EC | 0.5ml/liter, foliar spray |
| Organic | Baking soda solution | 5g/liter + few drops soap |

## Risk Factors
- Cool, humid weather
- Overcrowded planting
- Excessive nitrogen fertilization
- Susceptible varieties

# Wheat Rust

## Overview
Wheat rust is a fungal disease affecting wheat crops worldwide. Three forms exist: stem rust (most destructive), leaf rust (most common), and stripe rust (favors cool climates).

## Symptoms

**Stem Rust:**
- Small reddish-brown pustules on stems and leaf sheaths
- Pustules rupture epidermis, releasing orange-brown spores
- Severe infection causes stem weakening and lodging

**Leaf Rust:**
- Small circular reddish-brown pustules on upper leaf surface
- Lighter green halos around pustules
- Mostly affects upper canopy leaves

**Stripe Rust:**
- Yellow stripes on leaves running parallel to veins
- Small yellow-orange pustules arranged in stripes
- Appears earliest in the growing season

## Identification

1. Inspect leaves and stems for pustules
2. Check if pustules rub off as orange/brown powder (spores)
3. Note location: stems (stem rust), upper leaves (leaf rust), stripes (stripe rust)
4. Moisture + moderate temperature (15-25°C) favors outbreak

## Treatment

| Stage | Treatment | Application |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Early infection | Carbendazim 50% WP | 1g/liter, foliar spray |
| Moderate | Mancozeb 75% WP | 2.5g/liter, foliar spray |
| Severe | Propiconazole 25% EC | 0.5ml/liter, foliar spray |
| Organic | Neem oil | 5ml/liter, repeated every 7 days |
| Cultural | Remove infected debris | Destroy burnt residue |

## Risk Factors
- High humidity (>80%)
- Moderate temperatures (15-25°C)
- Dense planting reducing air circulation
- Overhead irrigation keeping foliage wet
- Susceptible varieties planted

## Escalation
- If pustules cover >50% of leaf area → recommend immediate fungicide
- If stem rust detected → escalate to expert (can cause total crop loss)
- If organic treatment fails after 2 applications → recommend conventional fungicide

# Cotton Bollworm

## Overview
The most devastating pest of cotton in India. Larvae feed on bolls, squares, and bolls, causing direct yield loss. Multiple generations per season.

## Symptoms
- Holes in bolls with frass inside
- Squares (flower buds) eaten at base
- Larvae visible inside damaged bolls
- Broken squares and damaged flowers
- Stunted bolls with chewed edges

## Identification
1. Inspect bolls for entry holes and frass
2. Check squares for feeding damage
3. Larvae are 30-40mm, variable color (green, pink, brown)
4. Dark stripes along body, hairy appearance

## Treatment
| Stage | Treatment | Application |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Light infestation | Spinosad 45% SC | 0.5ml/liter |
| Moderate | Emamectin benzoate 5% SG | 0.3g/liter |
| Severe | Chlorantraniliprole 18.5% SC | 0.4ml/liter |
| Organic | Neem oil + neem cake | 5ml/liter + 25kg/ha soil |
| Biological | Bt (Bacillus thuringiensis) | 2.5g/liter |
| Cultural | Pheromone traps | 10 traps/hectare |

## Life Cycle
1. Moth lays eggs on cotton foliage
2. Larvae hatch and feed on squares/bolls
3. 4-6 generations per season
4. Pupate in soil, emerge as moths

## Risk Factors
- Susceptible cotton varieties
- Delayed pest management
- High nitrogen fertilization
- Continuous cotton cropping
- Lack of natural predators

# Maize Stalk Borer

## Overview
Larvae bore into stalks and ears of maize, causing severe damage. Often called "shoot fly" or "stalk borer" in different regions.

## Symptoms
- Deadheart — central shoot dies and can be pulled out easily
- windows on leaves — pinhole entry points
- Frass (excrement) visible at entry holes
- Broken stalks at base
- Poor ear formation, empty cobs

## Identification
1. Check for pinhole entries on leaves
2. Pull dead central shoot — if larva present, confirmed
3. Look for frass at stem entry points
4. Larvae are creamy white with brown head, 25-30mm long

## Treatment
| Stage | Treatment | Application |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Early infestation | Chlorpyriphos 20% EC | 2.5ml/liter drench at stem base |
| Moderate | Carbofuran 3G | 10kg/hectare at base |
| Severe | Endosulfan 35% EC | 2ml/liter (use with caution) |
| Organic | Neem oil + chili garlic spray | 5ml/liter neem + 10g chili |
| Biological | Bacillus thuringiensis | 2.5g/liter spray on whorl |
| Cultural | Remove and destroy infested plants | Burn or deep bury |

## Life Cycle
1. Adult moth lays eggs on leaf surface
2. Larvae hatch and bore into whorl
3. Feed on developing shoot causing deadheart
4. Later bore into stalk and ears
5. Pupa in soil, emerge as moths

## Risk Factors
- Dense planting
- Delayed sowing
- Susceptible varieties
- Monsoon onset timing
- Weed presence in field

- Recommend resistant varieties (Bt corn) for next season

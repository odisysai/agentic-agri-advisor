# Rice Stem Borer

## Overview
Larvae bore into rice stems, causing "white head" (empty panicles) and deadheart. Major pest in irrigated rice ecosystems.

## Symptoms

**Deadheart:**
- Central shoot dies and can be pulled out
- Yellowing of central leaves
- Larva inside stem

**White Head:**
- Panicles fail to emerge
- White, empty panicles at heading stage
- Larva inside stem blocks grain development

## Identification
1. Pull dead central shoot — larva present inside
2. Cut stem longitudinally — larva visible
3. Larvae are creamy white, 20-25mm, cylindrical
4. Check for entry holes at stem base

## Treatment
| Stage | Treatment | Application |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Deadheart stage | Cartap hydrochloride 50% WP | 1g/liter drench |
| White head stage | Chlorpyriphos 20% EC | 2ml/liter |
| Moderate | Fipronil 5% SC | 1ml/liter |
| Organic | Neem oil + vermillion paste | 5ml/liter + apply paste at entry |
| Biological | fish peptide + neem | 5ml/liter |
| Cultural | Drain field, expose soil | Reduce humidity |

## Life Cycle
1. Moth lays eggs on rice leaves
2. Larvae hatch and bore into stem
3. Feed inside stem causing deadheart/white head
4. Pupa in stem, emerge as moths

## Risk Factors
- Uniform variety planting (large monoculture)
- Synchronized flowering
- High nitrogen
- Dense planting
- Flooded conditions

## Escalation
- If deadheart in >10% plants → immediate intervention needed
- If white head in >20% panicles → assess yield loss
- Recommend mixed varieties to reduce pest buildup
- Advise on water management to disrupt life cycle

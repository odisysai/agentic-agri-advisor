# Wheat Leaf Eating Caterpillar

## Overview
Caterpillars feed on wheat leaves, skeletonizing them and reducing photosynthetic capacity. Outbreaks can cause significant yield loss if not managed.

## Symptoms
- Irregular holes in leaves
- Skeletonized leaves (veins remaining)
- Greenish-brown caterpillars visible on plants
- Frass (excrement) on leaves and soil
- Defoliation in severe cases

## Identification
1. Look for caterpillars on leaf surfaces
2. Check for irregular leaf damage
3. Caterpillars are 30-40mm, greenish-brown
4. Active at night, hide during day

## Treatment
| Stage | Treatment | Application |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Light | Carbaryl 50% WP | 1.5g/liter |
| Moderate | Chlorpyriphos 20% EC | 2ml/liter |
| Severe | Lambda-cyhalothrin 5% EC | 0.5ml/liter |
| Organic | Bt (Bacillus thuringiensis) | 2.5g/liter |
| Organic | Neem oil spray | 5ml/liter |
| Cultural | Remove weeds from field margins | Reduce breeding sites |

## Life Cycle
1. Moth lays eggs on weeds/cereal crops
2. Larvae hatch and feed on leaves
3. Multiple generations possible
4. Pupate in soil

## Risk Factors
- Weed presence in and around fields
- Consecutive wheat cropping
- Delayed sowing
- Lack of natural predators
- Favorable weather (moderate temp + humidity)

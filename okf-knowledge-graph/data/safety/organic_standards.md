# Organic Farming Standards

## Overview
Organic farming prohibits synthetic chemicals and requires specific practices for pest and disease management.

## Approved Organic Treatments

| Treatment | Target | Application Rate | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|-------|
| Neem oil | Broad-spectrum pest control | 5 ml/liter | Most versatile organic option |
| Neem cake | Soil pest control | 25 kg/hectare | Also acts as fertilizer |
| Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt) | Caterpillars | 2.5 g/liter | Specific to Lepidoptera larvae |
| Trichoderma | Fungal diseases | 10 g/liter | Biocontrol agent |
| Pseudomonas fluorescens | Root diseases | 10 g/liter | Biocontrol for soil-borne pathogens |
| Copper sulfate (limited) | Fungal diseases | 2 g/liter | Allowed in limited quantities |
| Sulfur | Powdery mildew | 3 g/liter | Safe for organic certification |
| Garlic extract | Sucking pests | Crushed garlic in water | Traditional remedy |
| Cow urine | General pest deterrent | Diluted 1:10 | Traditional remedy |
| Wood ash | Soft-bodied insects | Dust on plants | Traditional remedy |

## Prohibited Substances

The following are NOT allowed in organic farming:
- Synthetic pesticides (carbendazim, chlorpyriphos, etc.)
- Synthetic fertilizers (urea, DAP, MOP)
- Genetically modified organisms (GMOs)
- Sewage sludge
- Irradiation

## Certification Requirements

### India (PGS-India)
- 3-year conversion period
- Document all inputs and practices
- Annual inspection by certification body
- Maintain field records

### International (EU, USDA, JAS)
- 2-3 year conversion period
- Strict documentation
- Annual third-party inspection
- Buffer zones between organic and conventional

## Organic Record Keeping

Farmers must maintain:
1. Field history (crops grown, inputs applied)
2. Input purchase records
3. Harvest records
4. Pest/disease observation logs
5. Treatment records (what, when, how much)

## When to Escalate

- If farmer asks about using synthetic chemicals → educate on alternatives
- If certification is needed → direct to certification body
- If organic treatment failing → suggest integrated approach

# Clay Soil

## Overview
Clay soil has very fine particles that pack tightly together. It retains water and nutrients well but has poor drainage and aeration. Common in black cotton soil regions of Maharashtra.

## Properties

| Property | Value | Implication |
|----------|-------|-------------|
| Particle Size | <0.002mm | Very fine, sticky when wet |
| Water Retention | High (60-70%) | Holds water but can waterlog |
| Drainage | Poor | Slow percolation, puddling risk |
| pH Range | 6.5-8.0 | Slightly alkaline |
| Organic Matter | 2-4% | Moderate, improves with practice |
| CEC (Cation Exchange Capacity) | High | Good nutrient holding capacity |
| Aeration | Poor | Limited oxygen for roots |
| Workability | When moist, not wet | Plow when soil crumbles, not sticky |
| Color | Dark brown to black | Rich in minerals |

## Water Management
- **Irrigation:** Drip irrigation preferred; avoid flood irrigation
- **Frequency:** Less frequent but deep watering
- **Timing:** Early morning or late evening
- **Volume:** 6-8 liters per plant per irrigation (depends on crop)
- **Caution:** Avoid waterlogging; ensure proper drainage channels

## Nutrient Management
- **Nitrogen:** Apply in split doses to prevent leaching
- **Phosphorus:** Fixed by clay; use phosphate rock or enhance with organic matter
- **Potassium:** Readily available but can be locked; use K-sulfate
- **Organic Matter:** Add compost/green manure regularly to improve structure

## Crop Suitability
**Good for:**
- Rice (paddy)
- Wheat
- Cotton (black cotton soil)
- Sugarcane
- Pomegranate
- Grapes

**Challenging for:**
- Root vegetables (carrot, beetroot)
- Legumes (poor drainage)
- Seedlings (crusting on surface)

## Amendments to Improve
1. **Organic matter** — compost, green manure, farmyard manure
2. **Gypsum** — improves structure in sodic clay soils
3. **Sand** — improves drainage (use sparingly)
4. **Biochar** — improves aeration and water holding
5. **Earthworms** — natural tillers, improve structure

## Crop-Specific Recommendations

### Wheat in Clay Soil
- **Sowing:** Late sowing (Nov) to avoid waterlogging
- **NPK:** N: 120 kg/ha, P: 60 kg/ha, K: 40 kg/ha
- **Irrigation:** 3-4 irrigations (critical stages: crown root initiation, tillering, flowering)
- **Drainage:** Create drainage channels between rows

### Rice in Clay Soil
- **Sowing:** Early kharif (June-July)
- **NPK:** N: 100 kg/ha, P: 40 kg/ha, K: 40 kg/ha
- **Irrigation:** Flood irrigation suitable (clay retains water)
- **Land preparation:** Puddled transplanting ideal

### Cotton in Clay Soil
- **Sowing:** After monsoon onset
- **NPK:** N: 120 kg/ha, P: 40 kg/ha, K: 60 kg/ha
- **Irrigation:** Drip preferred; avoid waterlogging at flowering
- **Weed management:** Critical in first 30 days

## When to Escalate
- If soil is sodic (high sodium, white patches) → recommend gypsum + leaching
- If waterlogging persists → recommend drainage infrastructure
- If pH >8.5 → recommend sulfur application to lower pH

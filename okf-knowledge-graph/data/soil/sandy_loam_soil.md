# Sandy Loam Soil

## Overview
Sandy loam has a mix of sand, silt, and clay particles. It drains well, warms up quickly, and is easy to work with. However, it has low nutrient and water holding capacity, requiring more frequent irrigation and fertilization.

## Properties

| Property | Value | Implication |
|----------|-------|-------------|
| Particle Size | 0.05-2mm | Gritty feel, drains well |
| Water Retention | Low to Moderate (30-40%) | Needs frequent light irrigation |
| Drainage | Good | No waterlogging risk |
| pH Range | 6.0-7.5 | Slightly acidic to neutral |
| Organic Matter | 1-3% | Low, needs regular addition |
| CEC | Low to Moderate | Nutrients leach easily |
| Aeration | Good | Healthy root oxygenation |
| Workability | Excellent | Easy to till, works when dry |
| Color | Light brown to yellowish | Lower organic content |

## Water Management
- **Irrigation:** Frequent, small volumes (drip ideal)
- **Frequency:** Every 3-4 days in summer, 5-7 days in winter
- **Timing:** Early morning preferred
- **Volume:** 4-6 liters per plant per irrigation (depends on crop)
- **Caution:** Avoid deep watering (water drains too fast)

## Nutrient Management
- **Nitrogen:** Apply in small, frequent splits (leaching risk)
- **Phosphorus:** Band placement near roots (less leaching)
- **Potassium:** Split applications, avoid heavy single doses
- **Organic Matter:** Add regularly — compost, green manure, mulch

## Crop Suitability
**Excellent for:**
- Cotton
- Groundnut (peanut)
- Potato
- Onion
- Mango
- Pomegranate
- Grapes

**Good for:**
- Wheat
- Corn
- Soybean
- Vegetables

**Challenging for:**
- Rice (needs waterlogging)
- Sugarcane (needs consistent moisture)

## Amendments to Improve
1. **Organic matter** — most important; compost, FYM, green manure
2. **Mulching** — reduces evaporation, adds organic matter
3. **Biochar** — improves water and nutrient holding
4. **Clay addition** — small amounts to improve water retention
5. **Cover crops** — prevent erosion, add organic matter

## Crop-Specific Recommendations

### Cotton in Sandy Loam
- **Sowing:** After monsoon onset or irrigated sowing
- **NPK:** N: 100 kg/ha, P: 50 kg/ha, K: 50 kg/ha (split doses)
- **Irrigation:** Drip critical; 4-5 irrigations per season
- **Mulching:** Recommended to conserve moisture

### Wheat in Sandy Loam
- **Sowing:** Timely sowing (Oct-Nov)
- **NPK:** N: 100 kg/ha, P: 50 kg/ha, K: 30 kg/ha (split N)
- **Irrigation:** 4-5 irrigations; don't skip critical stages
- **Mulching:** Straw mulch conserves moisture

### Corn in Sandy Loam
- **Sowing:** Pre-monsoon or irrigated
- **NPK:** N: 120 kg/ha (split 3 times), P: 60 kg/ha, K: 40 kg/ha
- **Irrigation:** Critical during silking; 5-6 irrigations
- **Fertilization:** Side dressing at 30 and 60 days

### Soybean in Sandy Loam
- **Sowing:** Early kharif
- **NPK:** N: 30 kg/ha (fixed by Rhizobium), P: 60 kg/ha, K: 40 kg/ha
- **Irrigation:** 2-3 irrigations if rain fails
- **Inoculation:** Rhizobium culture at sowing

## When to Escalate
- If severe erosion observed → recommend contour bunds, cover crops
- If salinity observed (white crust) → recommend leaching, gypsum
- If organic matter <1% → recommend green manure + FYM program

# Alluvial Soil

## Overview
Alluvial soil is deposited by rivers and is the most fertile soil type in India. Found in the Indo-Gangetic plains, it is rich in nutrients and supports diverse agriculture.

## Water Management
- **Irrigation:** Flexible — drip or flood both work
- **Frequency:** Every 5-7 days in summer, 7-10 days in winter
- **Timing:** Morning or evening
- **Volume:** 5-7 liters per plant per irrigation
- **Advantage:** Good natural water holding

## Nutrient Management
- **Nitrogen:** 80-120 kg/ha depending on crop
- **Phosphorus:** 40-60 kg/ha
- **Potassium:** 30-50 kg/ha
- **Organic Matter:** Maintain with regular additions
- **Note:** Naturally fertile but depletes with continuous cropping

## Crop Suitability
**Excellent for:**
- Rice
- Wheat
- Sugarcane
- Maize (corn)
- Sorghum (jowar)
- Pulses
- Vegetables

**Good for:**
- Cotton
- Oilseeds
- Fruits (mango, guava)

## Crop-Specific Recommendations

### Rice in Alluvial Soil
- **Sowing:** Kharif (June-July) or Rabi (Nov-Dec for transplanted)
- **NPK:** N: 100-120 kg/ha, P: 40-60 kg/ha, K: 40 kg/ha
- **Irrigation:** Flood irrigation ideal; 5-6 irrigations
- **Land prep:** Puddled transplanting or direct seeding

### Wheat in Alluvial Soil
- **Sowing:** Oct-Nov (timely sowing critical)
- **NPK:** N: 120 kg/ha, P: 60 kg/ha, K: 40 kg/ha
- **Irrigation:** 4-5 irrigations (critical: crown root, tillering, flowering, grain fill)
- **Bonus:** Fertile soil supports high yields

### Sugarcane in Alluvial Soil
- **Sowing:** Feb-March or Aug-Sept
- **NPK:** N: 200 kg/ha, P: 60 kg/ha, K: 80 kg/ha (high demand)
- **Irrigation:** 8-10 irrigations per season
- **Manuring:** 25-40 tonnes FYM per hectare

## When to Escalate
- If soil is saline (white crust, poor germination) → recommend leaching, gypsum
- If waterlogging observed in low areas → recommend drainage channels
- If soil acidity developing (pH <6.0) → recommend lime application
